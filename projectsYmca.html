YMCA Saskatoon

RWD Website Redesign

About the Project

There are many nonprofit and charitable organizations for people to choose from when looking to donate. The ability for users to make a donation quickly and easily is an important feature to consider for organizations that run off of donations.

The YMCA is a youth social organization that works to promote equity and boost the community's well-being.

Throughout our research into the YMCA Saskatoon website, we discovered problems with the website stemmed from users having an overall poor experience. Users expressed navigating the website was not easy, they felt unsure and confused about the YMCA's purpose and were not motivated to donate.

Our solution was to create a prototype with clean design, intuitive navigation and purposeful information. Through implementing these changes, our research shows users understood the YMCA purpose, they are more confident navigating the website and their experiences are overall more positive.

We conducted qualitative and quantitative interviews to gain insight into what motivates users to donate.

Our interviews uncovered three themes:
1. When looking to donate, users want to feel they can trust the organization
2. Users want to know where their money is being allocated
3. Users dd not understand the YMCA's purpose and lacked the motivation to donate due to their poor website experience

Some of our user quotes highlight how users don't trust the website or take it seriously, they don't understand the YMCA's purpose, and how the overall design of the website is challenging to look at.

“My head hurts just looking at the whole thing”
“The way the website is built, it's hard for me to take it seriously to make a donation”

User Research Interview
From our Affinity Diagram, we looked closely at our users' navigation experience. We noticed users felt confused, overwhelmed, unsure and frustrated when trying to understand and navigate the current YMCA website.
Affinity Diagram
Proto-Persona

After defining our user persona and analyzing the Affinity Diagram, we determined the User Insight.

Potential donors need organized and concise information before making a donation and committing to volunteer activities, because the credibility of an organization and transparency is important to them.

The YMCA website was designed to promote its services and encourage people to donate to their causes. However, we observed how challenging and overwhelming it is for people to navigate the YMCA Saskatoon website and understand their primary purpose.

So, we defined the Problem Statement as:

How might we improve the YMCA Saskatoon website so that potential donors understand the YMCA Mission through a better navigation experience and feel motivated to donate so that the organization is successful based on the number of website visits and donations made to the organization?

We brainstormed ideas to create design solutions using the I Like, I Wish, What If Method starting the Ideate Phase. We identified the most critical problems to be solved based on what would bring the most value to the user and organization.

Mapped on a Feature Prioritization Matrix, here are the High Impact/Low Complexity topics that we decided to move forward:

Prioritization Matrix (Hight Impact / Low Complexity)
  • The donation could be made in a few clicks
  • Good and clean design
  • They have multiple donation options
  • More meaningful images rather than too much text
  • Content is more minimal, concise and served a purpose
  • Information was better summarized

We've also created a Value Proposition to make sure the new website would meet the needs of our users. In our Value Proposition, we highlight:

  • The redesign of the YMCA website to help users understand how impactful and essential their support is
  • Our pride in being a credible community
  • They have multiple donation options
  • Our work to strengthen communities and improve the health and well-being of people of all ages

After additional inputs and analysis of the User Scenario, Storyboard and User Journey Map, we were ready to develop our user flow and prototypes.

Our team designed responsive web design wireframes, prioritizing: Registration, Donation, Purpose and Schedule sections. We considered the importance of concise and meaningful information and developed a simple flow that was more intuitive for our users.

We created a clickable Low Fidelity prototype and conducted a Usability Testing Plan. The key insights used for the improvement of the high fidelity prototype were:
  • Purpose and Donation Flow was clear and intuitive to the user on prototypes
  • Schedules needed to be adjusted to make the navigation easier
  • Registration forms needed some minor adjustments in terms of required fields, and labelling
  • Navigation was easy to understand and intuitive for users on mobile and desktop prototypes
With all the test observations noted, we developed our High Fidelity Prototype, adjusting some forms, labelling, and our schedule.
